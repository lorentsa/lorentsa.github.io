    About Meredith Grey

    My name is Meredith Grey. I am a character from the medical drama television series Grey's Anatomy.
    I am the Chief of General Surgery in Grey-Sloan Memorial Hospital in Seattle, Washington.
    In the series I struggle with the everyday life of being in a competitive profession, while having
    to deal with a dramatic love life and complex family. During my work in the hospital my colleagues
    became my family and helped me overcome the various tragedies in my life, which includes an airplane crash,
    drowning, the loss of my father Thatcher Grey, my beloved sister Lexie Grey, my brilliant mother Ellis Grey
    and the worst of them all – my spouse and life partner Derek Shepherd.
    I have three beautiful children which give me the power to get up in the morning despite the grief and sorrow.
    As the fifteenth season of my series is being aired my life gets more complicated, I engage in a new and
    exciting love affair, invent exciting new medical techniques and who knows what's more to come.

    Answers


    Three differences between web 1.0, web 2.0 and web 3.0:
    Static and Dynamic: the pages in web 1 are static pages – most of the time they remain the same or they are updated occasionally.
    On the other hand, in web 2 and web 3 the pages are dynamically and frequently update.

    Network analysis: In web 1 and web 2 the importance of analysis is not high.
    On the other hand, in web 3 the importance of analysis is high - there are options of analyzing data which very helps
    the users due to the huge amount of data.

    Interaction: web 1 allows us only to read data and not to enter new data – it is the “readable” phrase of the World Wide Web
    with flat data. Web 2 is the “writable” phrase of the World Wide Web with interactive data.
    It facilitates interaction between web users and sites, so it allows users to interact more freely with each other.
    In addition, it encourages participation, collaboration, and information sharing. Web 3 is the “executable” phrase of Word Wide Web with
    dynamic applications, interactive services, and “machine-to-machine” interaction. In Web 3.0, computers can interpret information
    like humans and intelligently generate and distribute useful content tailored to the needs of users.

    The use case: "The best aerobic sport to lose weight"
    Web 1.0: It will return static pages which most of them old and not relevant. If I will like to filter kinds of sport, I will not
    be able to do this.

    Web 2.0: It will return more relevant pages from coach's blogs, facebook and so more. If I will like to filter kinds of sports
    I will be able to do so. In addition, I can interact with other users and can ask them for more advices and feedback.

    Web 3.0: It will return more personal pages. The first pages will include relevant information for me, such as near coaches and
    swimming and running's trainning which I searched a lot in the past.
